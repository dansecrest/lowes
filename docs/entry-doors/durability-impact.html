Durability & Impact

Durability & Impact

From hurricane debris to the decibels of a passing airplane, there are ways to measure what can get through your door.

Things to consider while you shop

Cancel out the noise.

The number of panes, types of coating and layers of insulation all factor into how much noise can travel through your door. A door’s Sound Transmission Class (STC) rating tells you how many decibels make it past the threshold.

*Product availability varies upon region.

Keep your A/C inside.

Invest in your new entryway with protective weather strips on the top and sides and a sweep at the bottom. You can also invest in a best-in-class door sill for a strong seal that keeps moisture out and your air conditioning inside.

*Product availability varies upon region.

Be ready for Mother Nature.

If you live in a coastal area, your building code might require that your entry door be able to withstand impact from severe weather. Each impact-rated door, frame, and glass panel is reinforced so that damage from wind and debris is minimized.

From locks to fire-resistance, you don’t ever have to second guess your entry door’s security.
